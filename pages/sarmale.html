Sarmale

About

There is not a lot one can say about sarmale. They are little cabbage rolls filled with goodness. However, it takes a long ass while to make them so you better show this recipe to your babushka and let her handle serious business.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg minced beef meat
  • 200 grams rice
  • 4 onions finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 5 tablespoons sun-flower oil
  • 1 liter tomato juice
  • pickled cabbage leaves (ideally, you make your own)
  • pepper (cloves & crushed)
  • bay leaves
  • thyme
  • paprika powder
  • salt

How to

Heat up the oil in a pan. When hot, add the onion and salt to taste and cook until shiny, but don’t let it burn. Then mix in the rice. Add your spices and tomato paste and some water just to get the rice cooking. The mixture is done when the rice is half cooked.

Set the mixture aside and let it cool for a bit. Combine the cooled mixture with the meat and mix very well.

Prepare the pot your cooking your sarmale in by lining it with cabbage leaves. Once your pot is ready, you can start rolling sarmale. For the best result possible, follow this lovely lady’s tutorial.

Arrange the sarmale tightly in the pot. Add the pepper cloves, bay leaves and then cover the whole thing with some more cabbage leaves. Add the tomato juice and about 300 ml water. Cook with lid on medium heat for about an hour and a half.

You now have glorious sarmale. This is not optional, but mandatory: enjoy them with sour cream and bread from babushka or polenta if you want to feel fancy.
